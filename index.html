Sao Kya Seng

The Last Prince of Hsipaw


Sao Kya Seng and his family

Here's the life of Sao Kya Seng

  • 1924 - Born in Hsipaw, Federated Shan State, British Burma
  • 1949 - Went to study mining enginnering in Colorado,United State
  • 1953 - Married to Inge Eberhard an Austrian student
  • 1954 - Return to Burma to resume his role as the prince of Hsipaw
  • 1954-1962 - Serve as a member of
    • Chamber of Nationalities
    • Shan State Council
    • Secretary for the Association of Shan Princes.
    • During his rules, he gave out his own paddy field, experimemt agricultrual projects.
  • ~1954 - He refused a request from a colonel in Northern command to host a gambling event
  • 1955 - Tatmadaw forces deployed to Shan State to fight against Chinese Koumingtang troops
  • 1962 - Ne Win sized power and arrested all ethnic and political leaders
  • 1962 - He was rumored to be detained in Taunggyi and is believed to have died.
  • 1964 - His wife, Inge, fled to Austria with their two young daughter.

To this day, Sao Kya Seng was considered to be one of the national leaders who promoted federalism and democracy.

"There are millions of refugees hiding in Thailand and Bangladesh. Another five millions of people or so are displaced inside Burma, which is now called Myanmar by the military regime." - Inge Sargent, wife of Sao Kya Seng
